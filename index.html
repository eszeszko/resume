About

Hi, my name is Eva Szeszko and I am a computer science undergraduate student at the University of Michigan's College of Engineering. One thing to note is that I usually spell my name E-W-A as that is the Polish spelling. However, either spelling is fine by me! The Polish pronunciation is Eh/vuh.

As a computer scientist, my interests include web development, machine learning, and AI. I have been coding since I was in high school and am always expanding my knowledge and seeking out new projects whether they be school related or of personal interest. There is always more to be learned and I routinely go beyond the scope of my classwork to improve my coding skills through online coding practices, learn more through online coursework, and excel as a coder.

As a student and potential employee, I strive to work with my peers in a collaborative, creative, and problem-solving environment. I believe a team that works best is a team that communicates. I have applied that idea to all areas of my life by being involved on campus in clubs such as Girls in Electrical and Computer Science (GEECS), Society of Women Engineers (SWE), my sorority, Alpha Delta Pi where I serve as the Family Events Chair, or my project team where I lead the UI/UX subteam.

To learn more about my experiences, check out my work experience or projects.

Projects

LC2K Assembler

Constructed a program to read in LC2K instructions and convert them to machine code. Then, implemented a memory diagram program to showcase the effects of machine code instructions in real time. Submitted for EECS370 course.

Stock Market Exchange Simualtor

Simulated a stock market exchange using priority queues and linked lists. Submitted for EECS281 course.

SQL Imitator

SQL simulator equipped to create, delete, insert, and join queries using C++ and hash maps for efficient operations. Submitted for EECS281 course.

Handwriting Recognizer

Trained deep nets and neural networks using Julia language to distinguish own handwriting. Also analyzed accuracy of testing and training data by updating the number of iterations, batch size, and learning rate that the neural network utilized. Submitted for EECS298 course.

Euchre

Simulated a 4-person game of Euchre stressing the use of pointers, classes, structs, enums, and other intertwining data structures. Combined polymorphic players as well as human strategy to mimic a real card-game using object-oriented programming. Submitted for EECS280 course.

Domino's Profit Optimization

Collaborated with a team of 3 students to optimize daily profit for a Domino’s store by 20% and improved workplace operations to 75%. Submitted for ENGR100 course.

Santa's Presents Game

Designed fully-playable game where Santa drops presents into moving chimneys using a mix of PyGame and Python. Game was then replicated by my Juni students. Read more about my Juni Learning experience.
